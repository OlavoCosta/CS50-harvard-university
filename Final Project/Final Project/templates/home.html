WEEK 6

After a few long and massive weeks of C-oriented content, week 6 of CS50 provides us with a rest of content and information. We cannot deny that thanks to the level of complexity and the various ways of solving the activities of the previous weeks, python has become "easy", but as master David Malan informed us, when you have the knowledge and the idea of what is going on under the hood, the other languages become easier, as they have the same logic of a low-level language like the C language, changing only a little of its logic and most of its syntax. Python is a high-level, interpreted scripting, object-oriented, functional, dynamically typed, and strong programming language; its syntax makes it much easier as it does not have many minimalistic details like a low-level language.

WEEK 7

Of all the previous weeks, the one I like the most is week 7 of CS50. Studying and gaining knowledge about the SQL language pleased me and motivated me to pursue a career in the data field. In week 7, we learned how to create databases and their tables as well as their syntax and types, statements, constraints, functions and transactions. The SQL language is the best known resource for programmers to execute commands in relational databases. It was through it that we were able to create the exercises proposed by CS50 in week 7 and the following weeks, as well as perform data queries. In short, it is through SQL that we "talk" to the database.

WEEK 9

In the penultimate week of CS50, David Malan introduced me to a content that I never imagined I would see, the python framework called FLASK. This framework allows ease, practicality and clarity in web development codes, applied in a clean, coherent and correct way flask becomes very useful for developers who usually do monthly or even weekly maintenance on websites. With week 9 of CS50, we learned how to use the routes of each page of the application, the decorators their requests and their responses, clearly flask is one of the applications that I want to work a lot in the future, developing my knowledge with Python, SQL and Flask.
